Recently, I wrote an article about chromatohpores and how they can 'see' without eyes. Find it linked here. I make the case that this behaviour is cognitive [link]. 
There is a recognised distinction between low-level processes informed purely by bottom-up, scene-based information such as edge detection, and high-level processes that take account of global organization and involve long-range top-down, viewer-informed, decision-based analyses that combine low-level retinal input with prior knowledge to generate representation.
